# Sapota GTM — Massachusetts-first

Massachusetts is the whole world for the next 12 months. 78 coastal municipalities, one state CZM office, one dominant incumbent consultancy (Woods Hole Group), one very specific funding ecosystem. Go deep. Ladder the other states only after 10 MA towns are paid.

---

## Why MA-first is the right call

1. **Ground truth advantage.** MA CZM inventoried 9,543 structures in 2006 and 2015. No other US state has matched pairs at that scale. Our Layer 2 model literally cannot be retrained elsewhere without that data.
2. **Your home turf.** UMass Dartmouth alumnus, LAUTEC on Vineyard Wind, SeaAhead pilot candidate. Every warm intro is one degree away.
3. **Density.** 78 coastal municipalities in a 192-mile state. You can drive to every DPW director in a day. Try that in California.
4. **Funded demand.** MVP Program has allocated $60M+ across MA towns since 2017. MA DER's Dam & Seawall Repair Fund has paid out $40M+ on prioritized project lists — the literal deliverable you produce.
5. **Concentrated buyer.** Land MA CZM (one org) and you've got statewide distribution.

## State buyers

One state government, three divisions you actually care about.

### MA CZM (Coastal Zone Management) — EEA

**Warmest entry point.** They are already your data provider. They have publicly committed to a living inventory but do not have budget or staff to build one.

- **Role to target:** Director of Coastal Resilience + StormSmart Coasts program lead. The CZM Director and Deputy Director sit above them.
- **Meeting pitch:** "We turned your 2015 inventory into a living scoring system with 0.443 correlation against your own field ratings. We'd like 30 minutes to show you — and discuss what MA CZM would need to use this operationally."
- **Ask:** A warm introduction to three towns and permission to cite CZM data publicly.
- **Revenue path:** $95k–$150k/yr statewide subscription once you have 3 municipal references.

### MA DER (Division of Ecological Restoration) — EEA

Operates the Dam and Seawall Repair or Removal Program. Distributes the funds. They read prioritized lists every cycle.

- **Role to target:** Program manager, Dam & Seawall Repair Fund.
- **Meeting pitch:** "Your applicants spend consultant dollars producing the same prioritization data every cycle. We can deliver it standardized, statewide, continuously."
- **Revenue path:** Service contract ($40k–$80k) to provide standardized prioritization input to the grant review process.

### MA EEA (Executive Office of Energy and Environmental Affairs)

Parent org. Runs the MVP program. Way too senior for a cold email, but the right warm intro (via SeaAhead, MassCEC, or a sitting mayor) shortcuts everything below.

- **Target:** Assistant Secretary for Climate or Undersecretary for Environment.
- **Ask:** Recognition that Sapota is a qualified vendor for MVP Action Grant deliverables — unlocks every MVP town at once.

## MA plays, sequenced

### Play 1 — CZM reference call (Week 1)
Highest-leverage single move. MA CZM gave you the ground truth. Show them Layer 3 validation, ask for three municipal intros.

### Play 2 — Scituate close (Week 2–4)
Paid pilot at $7,500 or formal reference agreement. The pilot gives you the first case study; Scituate's coastal resilience officer becomes a peer-to-peer reference.

### Play 3 — SeaAhead Pilot 2026
Use SeaAhead as a forcing function — it makes Scituate's pilot non-hypothetical and gives you a cohort of investors and operators watching.

### Play 4 — MVP Action Grant co-application (Week 5–8)
Pick Hull or Marshfield. Ghost-write their MVP application with Sapota as deliverable. Applications due spring. Award comes with a six-figure line item named "Sapota."

### Play 5 — Woods Hole Group conversation (Week 6–8)
One meeting. Offer partnership. If no, you now know exactly what RFP language to counter. Either outcome is better than assuming.

### Play 6 — Engineering firm seat licenses (Week 8–12)
Two meetings. GEI and Weston & Sampson. $36k/yr each. These close faster than municipalities if you hit the right practice lead.

### Play 7 — NOAA SBIR Phase I submission
$175k non-dilutive. Applies anytime in cycle. Buys runway, adds credibility. Do not skip.

### Play 8 — NERCZMA / ASBPA conference talks
One speaker slot at Northeast Regional CZM Association puts you in front of every MA/RI/CT/NH CZM director simultaneously. The next play outside MA starts here.
